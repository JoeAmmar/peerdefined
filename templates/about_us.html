About Us




Our Mission


The mission of Peer Defined is to add clarity to peer-reviewed publications and unite different disciplines.

We invite students, faculty, staff, and other consumers of peer-reviewed publications to collect and submit terms defined in peer-reviewed publications to distribute it globally.



How Our Story
Began


It all started in the fall of 2017 when Joy was tasked to develop and present a moonshot project in Randy J. McCarthy’s Aggression class at Northern Illinois University; this project was supposed to put a dent into the field of psychology.

During that Aggression class, Joy and her fellow classmates noticed something—the definition of aggression constantly evolved. Other aggression related terms even differed from publication to publication. This ended up being one of the common critiques of many of the articles that they read.


This
Sparked
An
Idea

What if we had a www.dictionary.com but for terms as defined in peer-reviewed articles? And thus, the idea of www.PeerDefined.com was born. Although the idea existed, its concept had not been brought to life.

It was not until Joe heard about the concept of PeerDefined through the grapevine in the spring of 2018 that it started to become a reality. Joe, interested in all forms of coding, wanted to help create PeerDefined. Today, Joy and Joe work together to bring you the best version of www.PeerDefined.com.
